Dear Schuchardt,
I am ashamed to think that your letter is still unanswered. But I am now in a position to send you Silvan Evans’ (note 1) answer to some of your queries. As for myself I really know exceedingly little about the work but I have always had an idea that all concerning the MSS. is very misslich. I am afraid that these letters will not satisfy you and I am sorry I cannot find your letter now nor your article in the Romania, (note 2) which I have been wanting lately: I have put it so carefully by that I know not where to find it: but it will no doubt turn up some day when I do not want it so much. By the way I should be glad to have  the rest of your account of your sojourn in Wales. I have no new views on the Celts: I find that I was anticipated by Norris: (note 3) we differ from the current opinion in dividing the Celts into Continental (Gauls) and Insular which are subdivided into Kymric and Goidelic: that is all.
Mrs. Rhys joins with me in kind regards to you: Myfanwy would now be able to talk Welsh with you. Max Müller is trying to induce Stokes to come and compete with me for the Celtic professorship at Oxford.
Yours truly
John Rhys
Rhyl
13.5.76

John Rhys, Yswain,
Rhyl
Lester’s article on D. ab G. is in the “Westminster Review” for Oct. 1873 (N.S. No. 88). (note 4) Any dispatch from Simla? (note 5)
D. S. E.
12/5/76.
