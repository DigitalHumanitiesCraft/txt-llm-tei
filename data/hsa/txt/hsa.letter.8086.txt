Panjab Notes and Queries
 EDITOR’S OFFICE
 AMBÁLÁ, PANJÁB, INDIA , Dated 26-1-1884 
My dear Sir,
I shall be most happy to insert your pieces in P. N.& Q. (note 1) & also to insert in future any notes you may be so kind as to favor me with.
Col Kenney-Herbert, Military Secretary to the Government of Madras (note 2) has a very good knowledge of Butler English as known  at Madras (note 3) & as he possesses considerable literary skill he might be able to help you by direct correspondence.
You may observe that I lose no opportunity of inserting instances of corrupted English in my journal & (note 4) it is a subject I have always taken an interest in & thought more valuable than people usually suspected.
Some years ago I compiled a largish Dict. in MS. S. of corruption of English and had many hopes on the subjet in MS. but as they were  compiled in the early days of my philological studies & are therefore crude & not very learned & as these studies have since engrossed all my time I have dropped the subject to a great extent. If however my labours will be of any use to you please let me know & I’ll see what I can do towards helping you in yours.
I am however an habitually overworked man & I fear I cannot help you much.
I am yours truly (note 5)
R.C. Temple (note 6)

Captain R. C. Temple
Bengal Staff Corps, Fellow of the
Royal Geographical Society
Member oft he Royal Asiatic
Philological and Folklore Societies
the Anthropological Institute
and the Asiatic Society
of Bengal
The Legends of the
Panjab. In monthly
Numbers from August 1883 (note 7)
