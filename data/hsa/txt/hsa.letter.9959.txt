2 Middlehurst Calcutta
 Nov. 30.1883 
Dear Sir,
I am obliged for your letter of the 2nd October, &amp; I would gladly be of any Service to you. But I fear that at present I can render you but slight assistance. My duties in the Viceroy’s Council absorb all my time, and preclude  me from the once agreable studies in which you are interested. The following, however, is a list of gentlemen who might perhaps be able to help you in regard to your researches in India.
Dr. Hoernle, The Madrissa, - Calcutta. (note 1)
J. Beames Esq, Commissioner
of Burdwan, Bengal. (note 2)
- Dr Forchhammer, Professor of Pali, (Burma), Rangoon (note 3)
- Captain de Loessoe, Foreign Office, Calcutta. (note 4)
- J. Jardine, Esq., Judicial Commissioner, Rangoon. (note 5)
- Denzil Ibbetson Esq. Lahore, Punjab. (note 6)
- Caplain R. Temple, Amballa, Punjab. (note 7) [This gentleman is editor of the journal The Indian Notes &amp; Queries, through which might be collected valuable facts.]
His Excelleny the Rv Hon’ble Grant Duff, (note 8) Governer of Madra
- I. Campbell, Esq., Editor of the Bombay Gazetteer, Bombay. (note 9)
- Dr. Burgess, Editor of the Indian Antiquary, Bombay. (note 10)
- Dr. Fonseca of Goa, Bombay (note 11)
- The Rev.dW. Miller, Principal of the Madras Christian College, Madras (note 12)
- Bishop 
Caldwell, Tinne-Vally, Madras (note 13)
- Lewis Rice Esq., Director  of Public Instruction, Mysore. (note 14)
I think that this list completes a circle of the Indian Province; &amp; I shall be glad if it proves useful to you.
I have perused your works with much interest; I regret that I cannot be more actively of service to you. But in  India the daily demands of administration leave now but little leisure for any pursuit not directly connected with administration work.
Wishing you well in your undertaking,

I am v[er]y faithfully Yours

Low Hunter
Dr. Hugo Schuchardt
